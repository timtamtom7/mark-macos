# Mark — Onboarding

4 screens for the screenshot annotation tool.

---

## Screen 1 — Welcome to Mark

**Headline:** Annotate screenshots in seconds.

**Body:** Mark makes it fast to capture, mark up, and share screenshots — arrows, shapes, text, blur, highlights, and more. No clutter, just the tools you need.

**Illustration:** A flat, energetic illustration showing a Mac screenshot frame on the left, being "marked up" with orange annotation elements on the right — an arrow pointing at something, a text label, and a highlight marker. The orange brand color pops against a clean light background. Feels quick and purposeful.

**CTA Button:** "Take a Screenshot →"

**Skip link:** "Skip setup" (bottom right)

---

## Screen 2 — Set Your Shortcut

**Headline:** Pick your capture shortcut.

**Body:** Press a key combination to instantly capture your screen. The default is `⌘⇧4` (like macOS screenshot), but you can customize it.

**Illustration:** A keyboard mockup showing the `⌘`, `⇧`, and `4` keys highlighted in orange. Below, a small "M" key label shows Mark's custom shortcut option. A dotted outline around the whole combination. Clean, instructional.

**CTA Button:** "Use ⌘⇧4" (default)

**Secondary link:** "Customize shortcut…"

---

## Screen 3 — Your Annotation Toolkit

**Headline:** Everything you need, nothing you don't.

**Body:** Mark comes with 9 annotation tools — each one a single tap away. Arrow, rectangle, ellipse, line, text, highlight, blur, freehand, and number stamps.

**Illustration:** A horizontal strip showing all 9 tool icons in a grid: Arrow (↗), Rectangle (□), Ellipse (○), Line (/), Text (T), Highlight (marker), Blur (pixelate grid), Freehand (squiggle), Number stamp (#). Each tool icon in a small rounded square. Orange borders on hover states. The Arrow tool is highlighted with an orange background, indicating it's the default.

**CTA Button:** "Next →"

---

## Screen 4 — Ready to Mark

**Headline:** You're all set.

**Body:** Press `⌘⇧4` to capture. Mark opens instantly with your screenshot ready to annotate.

**Illustration:** A clean, confident illustration: a Mark toolbar (orange bar with tool icons) above a screenshot canvas showing a captured browser window. An arrow annotation is mid-draw, pointing at the browser's address bar. The arrow is in orange. A subtle "tap to mark" hint text in the corner. The overall feel: fast, ready, get-to-work.

**CTA Button:** "Open Mark"
